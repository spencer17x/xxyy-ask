# Filter

A well-configured filter is the key to making your on-chain analysis faster and more effective. Our powerful filter panel allows you to cut through the noise and pinpoint projects that match your exact criteria.

You can apply a wide range of conditions, including:

* Filter by Protocol: Select single or multiple protocols to narrow your search to specific launch platforms like pump.fun, LetsBonk, and others.
* Filter by Social Media & ADs: Require projects to have an online presence by filtering for those that have a Twitter, Telegram, or Website link. You can also specifically look for projects that have paid for advertising on Dexscreener.
* Filter by Keywords: Use the Search Keywords and Excluded Keywords fields to include or remove tokens based on their name or ticker.
* Filter by On-Chain Metrics: Under the General tab, you can set precise ranges for a variety of on-chain data points, such as:
  * Creation Time (in minutes)
  * Market Cap
  * Number of Holders
  * Liquidity
  * Bonding Curve %

Launch Alert

To ensure you never miss a critical launch, you can enable the Launch Alert.

This is an audible notification that will play a sound whenever a project from the Final Stretch list is about to complete its funding and migrate to a DEX. With this feature enabled, you can stay on top of every opportunity without having to watch the screen constantly.

Holdings

If you hold the token you are currently viewing, this panel will automatically appear, providing a detailed breakdown of your investment performance.

* Invested: The total USD value of all your buy transactions for this token, along with the total quantity purchased and the number of trades.
* Sold: The total USD value you have received from selling this token, along with the total quantity sold and the number of trades.
* Remaining: The current market value of the tokens you still hold.
* P\&L (Profit & Loss): Displays your net profit or loss as a percentage, calculated as: (Remaining Value + Sold Value - Invested Value) / Invested Value.
